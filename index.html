About Me

My name is Shelly Bindra, and I am a psychologist-neurologist. I graduated from the Berlin Medical Academy - Vorsemester Medizin and have over 30 years of experience in the field. I am passionate about understanding the human mind and helping individuals improve their mental well-being.

Throughout my career, I have acquired various certifications related to psychology, including:

  • Certificate in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
  • Advanced Certification in Neuropsychology
  • Certified Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) Practitioner
  • Certification in Trauma-Informed Care

I believe in a holistic approach to mental health, combining my expertise in psychology and neurology to provide comprehensive care to my patients. My goal is to create a safe and supportive environment where individuals can explore their emotions, gain insights, and develop effective strategies to overcome challenges.

Areas of Interest

Anxiety

Feeling of apprehension and excessive concern about the future, not knowing how to deal with uncertainties, often accompanied by physical symptoms such as sweating, racing heart, tremors, nausea, shortness of breath, chest pain, insomnia, etc. It is normal to feel anxiety in stressful or challenging situations, but when it is constant or excessive, it can become problematic anxiety and interfere with daily life, affecting personal well-being.

Stress

It is a natural reaction of the body and mind to situations perceived as threatening or challenging. When it becomes chronic and intense, it can have a significant impact on physical and mental health. It can lead to a number of problems such as depression, anxiety, post-traumatic stress disorder and sleep disorders. It can affect the ability to concentrate, memory and decision making.

Depression

Depression is a mental disorder that affects mood, thinking and behavior, compromising the person's quality of life, caused by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness and lack of interest in activities that were previously pleasurable, among other factors. In addition, depression can cause changes in appetite, sleep, concentration and energy level, etc. Meanwhile different from what many imagine depression can present itself in different ways for each person

Fear and Insecurity

Lack of confidence in yourself or your abilities can be caused by several factors, such as traumatic experiences, low self-esteem, lack of social skills, decision-making, among others. Insecurity can affect many areas of life, such as personal relationships, professional career and social life.

Burnout

It is a state of physical, emotional and mental exhaustion that can occur in people who face chronic stress in their work, lack of a healthy work-life balance. It is characterized by emotional exhaustion, feeling disconnected from others and work, low personal fulfillment, irritability, fatigue, among other factors.
